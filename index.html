Tomato Detection

In our study, we focused on the accurate detection of tomatoes grown in greenhouses, which is crucial for timely harvesting. We aimed to differentiate ripe tomatoes from unripe ones, addressing challenges like insufficient light, occlusion, and overlapping that can hinder detection. Recognizing that manual detection in large greenhouses is time-consuming, we explored high-performance automatic detection methods suitable for greenhouse robots.

We compared two feature extraction techniques: Histogram of Oriented Gradients (HOG) and Local Binary Patterns (LBP), alongside two popular machine learning classifiers, Support Vector Machines (SVM) and k-Nearest Neighbors (kNN). We evaluated the performance of HOG and LBP features both individually and in combination, analyzing the effectiveness of each approach.

To further enhance detection accuracy, we implemented a post-processing stage that utilized color information to reduce false positives. This comprehensive approach allowed for improved tomato detection and counting, ultimately contributing to more efficient greenhouse management.

Electrical Powered Three Wheel Car

This project emphasizes the importance of alternative energy vehicles by focusing on the development of an eco-friendly electric vehicle. It aims to address environmental issues, given the tripling of vehicle numbers in our country from 2001 to 2022 and the fact that electric and hybrid vehicles comprise only 10.7% of the total. In this context, a vehicle was designed to compete in the Shell Eco-marathon, which showcases energy-efficient alternative fuel vehicles. The project seeks to set a pioneering example in terms of both vehicle design and energy efficiency.

In this project, where I primarily worked in systems engineering and project management, I played an active role in various phases, including writing technical specifications, participating in procurement tenders, developing the project plan, and overseeing compliance throughout the process, as well as controlling service acquisitions, evaluating project outputs, and conducting system tests. As a result, we successfully completed the project with a functional system and documented our findings. Additionally, I contributed to coding activities for creating a prototype that displayed information related to the vehicle, such as speed, time, and remaining battery life features that were not part of the initial project plan. This prototype was developed using a Raspberry Pi 4, integrating a display and sensors into the system design.
